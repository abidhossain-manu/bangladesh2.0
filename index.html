Bangladesh 2.0

Idea, innovation, Vision and challenges in New Bangladesh`

Pillers of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh fosters innovation through technology, startups, renewable energy, and education.

Sustainability

Bangladesh promotes sustainability via green energy, eco-friendly practices, and reforestation.

Infrastructure

Bangladesh develops infrastructure through bridges, highways, urbanization, and smart cities.

Empowerment

Bangladesh empowers communities through education, microfinance, entrepreneurship, and women's rights.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation’s trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

Each of us has unlimited potential, and our ability to unleash this potential depends on the opportunities and support we receive. Poverty is not created by the poor; it is created by the systems, institutions, and policies we have designed. We must create a world where no one is left behind, where businesses exist for social impact, not just profit. The goal of social business is to solve problems, empower communities, and build sustainable systems. Wealth concentration is a global issue; we need to democratize opportunities. By empowering women and marginalized groups, we create pathways to growth and prosperity. The power of one person to change the world is infinite. Dreams fuel action, and action transforms lives. A sustainable future begins with shared responsibility, innovation, and the courage to challenge norms.

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
